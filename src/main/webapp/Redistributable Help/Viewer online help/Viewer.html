The iXv Viewer visualizes the real-world objects stored in data sources – such as companies, locations, or people – as pictorial entities in a web browser. Descriptive links between entities represent relationships between objects in the data source, such as ownership or shareholding.

The picture the iXv Viewer paints is called a chart.

For example, the chart above is a succinct visual summary of the information a data source holds about a particular person. You can instantly see how other objects are related, and get key facts such as telephone numbers or addresses at your fingertips.

A chart can also be interactive, in that you may be able to:

  • drill down and add more related entities to the chart, to explore the data source visually

  • access additional information about entities stored in the data source, such as the source document behind a financial transaction

In addition, you can transfer charts to desktop applications to analyze and distribute the collated information, while continuing to interact with the data source online.

Because data is more readily comprehensible when represented pictorially, and because visualization uncovers relationships that may not otherwise have been apparent (or even accessible) in results laid out as tables of text, the iXv Viewer helps you turn information into actionable intelligence.
